Area & Units Explorer

Visualize first. The equation always comes second.
★ What does 1 m² really mean?
It is the area enclosed by a square that is 1 m wide and 1 m tall. The "1" in 1 m² is hidden — the unit comes from m × m = m².
1 × 1 = 1   AND   m × m = m²
→ 1 m² is one little square. Count how many fit inside any shape — that's the area.
★ Multiplication IS area
Drag the sliders. The rectangle fills with b × h unit squares — that count is the area. The unit is (unit × unit) = unit².
Area
20 m²
5 m × 4 m = 20 m²
Unit Math
m × m = m²
The unit comes from the multiplication.
★ A parallelogram is just a rectangle in disguise
Press Slide! — the slanted triangle slides from one side to the other. The shape becomes a rectangle of the same base & perpendicular height. Same area. Formula: $\text{Area} = b \times h$   (use the perpendicular $h$, never the slant).
Area
40 m²
Slant doesn't matter. The perpendicular height does.
★ Two trapezoids make a parallelogram
Press Flip & Match! — a copy of the trapezoid flips upside down and fits next to the original. Together they form a parallelogram with base $b_1 + b_2$ and height $h$. So one trapezoid is half: $$\text{Area}_\text{trap} = \dfrac{(b_1 + b_2) \times h}{2}$$
Area
30 m²
(4 + 8) × 5 / 2 = 60 / 2 = 30

★ Concept #1 — What does "m²" really mean?
1 m² = the area enclosed by a 1 m × 1 m square. Look at the multiplication closely: $$ \underbrace{1}_{hidden} \times \underbrace{1}_{hidden} = 1 \qquad \text{and} \qquad m \times m = m^2 $$ So when you write 5 m × 6 m = 30 m², the unit isn't decoration — it comes from the multiplication itself. Numbers multiply with numbers, units multiply with units.
A square that is 1 m wide and 1 m tall holds exactly 1 m² of area. Bigger shapes? Just count how many of those 1 m² squares fit inside.
★ Concept #2 — Multiplication IS area (Rectangle)
A rectangle of base $b$ and height $h$ holds $b \times h$ unit squares. That product — number AND unit — is the area. Arearect = b × h

★ Concept #3 — Triangle is HALF of a rectangle/parallelogram
Take any triangle. Make a copy and flip it upside-down → the two pieces form a parallelogram of the same base & height. So one triangle is half of that.
The dashed triangle is the "flipped copy". Together they make a rectangle of $b \times h$. So: Areatri = (b × h) / 2 Important: $h$ is the perpendicular height — NOT the slanted side.

★ Concept #5 — Trapezoid: DON'T memorize, DRAW it
Take any trapezoid. Make an identical copy, flip it upside down, and place it next to the original → the two together form a parallelogram with base $(b_1 + b_2)$ and height $h$. One trapezoid is half of that.
The parallelogram has base $b_1+b_2$ and height $h$, so its area is $(b_1+b_2)\cdot h$. One trapezoid is half: Areatrap = (b₁ + b₂) × h / 2

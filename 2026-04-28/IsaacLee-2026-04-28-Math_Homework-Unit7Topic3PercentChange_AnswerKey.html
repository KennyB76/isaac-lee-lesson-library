Part C — Percent of Change MEDIUM
Problem 5
40 points to 60 points. Increase or decrease? Percent of change?
Percent Increase
Change: \(60 - 40 = 20\) (went up → increase).
Set up: \(\dfrac{20}{40} = \dfrac{X}{100}\).
Solve: \(X = \dfrac{20 \times 100}{40} = 50\).
50% Increase
Problem 6
$80 to $60. Increase or decrease? Percent of change?
Percent Decrease
Change: \(\$80 - \$60 = \$20\) (went down → decrease).
Set up: \(\dfrac{20}{80} = \dfrac{X}{100}\) ⇒ \(X = 25\).
25% Decrease
Problem 7
25 points to 50 points. Increase or decrease? Percent of change?
Percent Increase Doubled
Change: \(50 - 25 = 25\) (increase).
Set up: \(\dfrac{25}{25} = \dfrac{X}{100}\) ⇒ \(X = 100\).
Note: When something doubles, the change equals the original — that's exactly 100% increase.
100% Increase
Problem 8
125 invitations to 75 invitations. Increase or decrease? Percent of change?
Percent Decrease

Set up: \(\dfrac{50}{125} = \dfrac{X}{100}\) ⇒ \(X = 40\).
40% Decrease
Part D — Find the Original Amount (Reverse) MEDIUM
Problem 9
After 20% off, shoes sell for $40. Find original price.
Reverse Discount 80% remains
After 20% off, sale price = 80% of original.
Equation: \(0.80 \times x = 40\).
\(x = \dfrac{40}{0.80} = \$50\).
$50
Problem 10
Including 10% sales tax, hat costs $44. Find price before tax.
Reverse Tax 110%
Total = 110% of original. Equation: \(1.10 \times x = 44\).
\(x = \dfrac{44}{1.10} = \$40\).
$40

Part G — Multi-Step Word Problems CHALLENGE
Problem 15
Bicycle $200. 25% off, then 6% tax on discounted price. Find final total.
Discount → Tax
Step 1 — Discount: \(0.25 \times \$200 = \$50\). Sale: \(\$200 - \$50 = \$150\).
Step 2 — Tax on sale: \(0.06 \times \$150 = \$9\).
Step 3 — Final: \(\$150 + \$9 = \$159\).
$159
Problem 16
Guitar $300. 30% off, then 10% tax on discounted price. Find final total.
Discount → Tax
Step 1 — Discount: \(0.30 \times \$300 = \$90\). Sale: \(\$300 - \$90 = \$210\).
Step 2 — Tax: \(0.10 \times \$210 = \$21\).
Step 3 — Final: \(\$210 + \$21 = \$231\).
$231
Problem 17
Book on sale for 30% off, you pay $63. Find original price.
Reverse Discount 70% remains
After 30% off, you pay 70% of original.
Equation: \(0.70 \times x = 63\) ⇒ \(x = \dfrac{63}{0.70} = \$90\).
Check: 30% of $90 = $27; $90 − $27 = $63 ✓.
$90
Problem 18
72 speeding tickets to 36. Increase or decrease? Find percent of change.
Percent Decrease Halved
Change: \(72 - 36 = 36\) (decrease).
Set up: \(\dfrac{36}{72} = \dfrac{X}{100}\) ⇒ \(X = 50\).
Note: Halving = 50% decrease.
50% Decrease
Problem 19
Store A: $50, 30% off. Store B: $60, 40% off. Which has the lower sale price, by how much?
Compare Better Buy
Store A: Discount = \(0.30 \times \$50 = \$15\). Sale: \(\$50 - \$15 = \$35\).
Store B: Discount = \(0.40 \times \$60 = \$24\). Sale: \(\$60 - \$24 = \$36\).
Compare: \(\$36 - \$35 = \$1\). Store A is cheaper by $1.
Lesson: A bigger discount % doesn't always win — the original price matters too.
Store A wins by $1 (Sale = $35)
Problem 20
Jacket $200. 25% off, then extra 10% off the sale price (coupon). Find final price.
Stacked Discounts
Step 1 — First discount (25%): \(0.25 \times \$200 = \$50\). Sale: \(\$200 - \$50 = \$150\).
Step 2 — Coupon (10% off the new $150): \(0.10 \times \$150 = \$15\). New price: \(\$150 - \$15 = \$135\).
Trap to avoid: 25% + 10% is NOT the same as 35% off ($130). The coupon is taken on the already-discounted price, not on the original. Stacked discounts always come out higher than the simple sum.
$135

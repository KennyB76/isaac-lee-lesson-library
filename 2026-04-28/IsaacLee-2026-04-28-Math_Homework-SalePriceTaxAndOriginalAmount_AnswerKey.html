Sale Price, Sales Tax, and Finding the Original Amount

Part A — Find the Sale Price
Problem 1
A jacket originally costs $90 and is on sale for 30% off. Find the sale price.
Discount
Discount: \(0.30 \times \$90 = \$27\).
Sale price: \(\$90 - \$27 = \$63\).
$63

Discount

Sale price: \(\$120 - \$42 = \$78\).
$78

Part C — Find the Original Price from the Sale Price
Problem 9
After 25% off, a shirt costs $60. What was the original price?
Reverse 75% remains
After 25% off, you pay \(100\% - 25\% = 75\%\) of the original.
Equation: \(0.75 \times x = 60\).
\(x = \dfrac{60}{0.75} = \$80\).
Check: 25% of $80 = $20; $80 − $20 = $60 ✓.
$80
Problem 10
After 20% off, a watch sells for $40. What was the original price?
Reverse 80% remains
After 20% off, sale = 80% of original. Equation: \(0.80 \times x = 40\).
\(x = \dfrac{40}{0.80} = \$50\).
Check: 20% of $50 = $10; $50 − $10 = $40 ✓.
$50
Problem 11
After 50% off, a backpack costs $35. What was the original price?
Reverse Half-price
After 50% off, sale = 50% of original (half).
Equation: \(0.50 \times x = 35\)  \(\Rightarrow\)   \(x = 35 \times 2 = \$70\).
$70
Problem 12
A book is on sale for 10% off and now costs $27. What was the original price?
Reverse 90% remains
After 10% off, sale = 90% of original. Equation: \(0.90 \times x = 27\).
\(x = \dfrac{27}{0.90} = \$30\).
$30
Part D — Find the Original Price from the Total Cost
Problem 13
Including a 5% sales tax, a meal costs $63. What was the price before tax?
Reverse Tax 105%
Total = original + 5% tax = 105% of original.
Equation: \(1.05 \times x = 63\).
\(x = \dfrac{63}{1.05} = \$60\).
Check: 5% of $60 = $3; $60 + $3 = $63 ✓.
$60

$40

Part E — Find the Rate
Problem 17
A toy was originally $80 and is now on sale for $60. What is the discount rate (% off)?
Find Rate Discount
Discount amount: \(\$80 - \$60 = \$20\).
Rate: \(\dfrac{20}{80} = 0.25 = 25\%\).
25%
Problem 18
A pen was originally priced at $20, and the total cost with sales tax was $21. What is the sales tax rate?
Find Rate Tax
Tax amount: \(\$21 - \$20 = \$1\).
Rate: \(\dfrac{1}{20} = 0.05 = 5\%\).
5%
Part F — Multi-Step (Discount, Then Tax)
Problem 19
A sweater is originally priced at $80 and is on sale for 25% off. After the discount, a 10% sales tax is applied. Find the final total cost.
Multi-Step Discount → Tax
Step 1 — Discount: \(0.25 \times \$80 = \$20\). Sale price: \(\$80 - \$20 = \$60\).
Step 2 — Tax on sale price: \(0.10 \times \$60 = \$6\).
Step 3 — Final total: \(\$60 + \$6 = \$66\).
$66
Problem 20
A skateboard regularly costs $100. The store offers 40% off, then adds a 5% sales tax to the discounted price. Find the final total cost.
Multi-Step Discount → Tax
Step 1 — Discount: \(0.40 \times \$100 = \$40\). Sale price: \(\$100 - \$40 = \$60\).
Step 2 — Tax on sale price: \(0.05 \times \$60 = \$3\).
Step 3 — Final total: \(\$60 + \$3 = \$63\).
$63

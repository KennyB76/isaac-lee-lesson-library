🔔 Mr. Kenny's Key Point

Cut it into triangles! Pick one vertex and draw all the diagonals — you get \((n - 2)\) triangles. Each triangle has 180°, so the polygon's interior angles add up to \((n - 2) \times 180°\).

Or remember: all exterior angles ALWAYS add to 360°.
